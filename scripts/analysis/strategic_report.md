# Delhi Capitals: The Road to Redemption (2026 Strategy)

## Phase 1: The Diagnosis
Why did we fail in 2025? The data points to four critical failures: the middle-over slump, opening instability, a death bowling crisis, and a lack of powerplay wickets.

### 1. The Middle-Over Slump (Overs 7-15)
Our middle order failed to capitalize compared to the league average.
![Middle Overs Analysis](../../results/analysis/strategic/diagnosis_middle_overs.png)
*   **Insight**: DC ranked poorly in scoring rate against both spin and pace in the middle overs.
*   [Interactive Graph](../../results/analysis/strategic/diagnosis_middle_overs.html)

### 2. Opening Partnership Instability
We constantly chopped and changed our opening pair, leading to poor performance.
![Opening Stability](../../results/analysis/strategic/diagnosis_opening_stability.png)
*   **Insight**: DC is in the "Danger Zone" (High Instability, Low Performance), using far more unique opening pairs than successful teams.
*   [Interactive Graph](../../results/analysis/strategic/diagnosis_opening_stability.html)

### 3. The Bowling Crisis (Death Overs)
Our death bowling was a major liability.
![Death Bowling](../../results/analysis/strategic/diagnosis_death_bowling.png)
*   **Insight**: DC had one of the highest economy rates in the death overs, leaking runs when it mattered most.
*   [Interactive Graph](../../results/analysis/strategic/diagnosis_death_bowling.html)

### 4. The Powerplay Bowling Void
We failed to take wickets with the new ball, putting pressure on the middle overs.
![Powerplay Bowling](../../results/analysis/strategic/diagnosis_pp_bowling.png)
*   **Insight**: DC ranked near the bottom for total Powerplay wickets.
*   [Interactive Graph](../../results/analysis/strategic/diagnosis_pp_bowling.html)
